Current Research

Proximity Induced Magnetization in 2D Materials

Carnegie Mellon University• November 2019 - Present

I am currently investigating proximity effects in 2D materials, specifically induced ferromagnetism in a typically non-magnetic conductor. We are fabricating devices using WTe2 as a thin non-magnetic conductor and coupling it with CrCl3 which is a ferromagnetic insulator. We plan on performing magneto-transport measurements on these devices to determine if magnetization is induced in the WTe2.
